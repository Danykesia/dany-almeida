Overview

Heavy Metal fans are a group of people used to consuming, for example, magazines, posters, CDs, t-shirts and reading online content, such as album reviews, band interviews, concert updates and news.

Based on this, this project aims to cater to Metal enthusiasts, providing them with a visually striking and easy-to-use platform to discover and interact, strengthen the community, promote events, and share content related to their favorite musical genre.

Why to do this project?

As a lover of music and Heavy Metal, it is a pleasure to explore this universe even more and discover how to improve the user experience on this type of content.

Furthermore, creating a blog for heavy metal fans is a unique and exciting approach, as it combines my passion for this music genre with my knowledge of user-centered design.

THE CHALLENGE

How might we create a design that authentically represents Heavy Metal culture, engages the target audience, and effectively showcases diverse content types while ensuring a user-friendly and responsive experience?

Balancing these aspects will be vital to the project's success.

Design Approach

To begin with, I used the design thinking process to understand the problem, generate ideas, develop designs, and iterate until I found a satisfactory solution. The great advantage of this process is that it is flexible and non-linear. That is, throughout each project stage, I can find new challenges, solutions, and insights about users.

Research

Benchmarking

I started the research by benchmarking 5 famous music blogs, of which 3 are direct competitors and 2 are indirect, to analyze, compare, and develop insights from the valuable data I obtained.

Therefore, I started this step by pasting screenshots of the pages into Figjam, taking note of the features that caught my attention the most, and evaluating each blog considering aspects such as:

  • Weaknesses
  • Strengths
  • Key blogging elements
  • Features

These aspects were essential to discovering the best practices of successful blogs, without which they could not develop satisfactory user engagement and even create a bad experience.

These were the quick insights and findings I extracted from the research:

Famous blogs use newsletters to build a database of readers and maintain direct communication with them, which makes it possible to increase the potential for recurring traffic.

Although only one of the blogs analyzed used the comments feature on posts (MetalSucks), I could identify how much this contributed to user engagement and interaction between them.

I must create a design that differentiates itself from the others in the sense of being able to represent the musical genre without exaggerating the intense and dark design that is typical in this type of blog.

USER INTERVIEW

To discover pain points and build empathy with users, I interviewed 4 Heavy Metal enthusiasts aged 18 and 45.

Using open-ended questions, I extracted from the participants a feeling of excitement about searching for content from the music scene that they like and, simultaneously, dissatisfaction with existing websites.

When I visit a Heavy Metal blog, I usually look for album reviews and band interviews. I enjoy getting insights into new releases and hearing other fans' perspectives.

The information structure isn't always intuitive, and finding what I'm looking for can become a slightly confusing task. It would be great if the navigation were more straightforward.

I usually find out about Heavy Metal on social media, following bands and artists, participating in online forums, and, of course, visiting blogs and websites dedicated to Heavy Metal to get news and reviews.

Many blogs aren't optimized for smaller screens, which can make reading and browsing less comfortable. A good mobile experience is crucial to me.

From here, the problem starts to become clear, and I realize that my main objective is to prioritize ease of navigation, encourage community interaction, and ensure accessibility on mobile devices without leaving aside the visual authenticity of the musical genre.

  • INSPIRATION

    • Firstly, I wanted to use a symbol that represents Heavy Metal culture. And I thought a hammer could represent the letter T.

    But, why a hammer, specifically Thor's Hammer (Mjolnir)?

    The answer is simple! Heavy Metal is heavily influenced by Nordic mythology, and this can be seen through the themes and influences that many bands incorporate in their music, lyrics, images, and live performances.

    As for the typography, I chose the Bowlby One SC font because it has thick, angular letters that convey impact, personality, clarity, and objectivity. Therefore, combining these factors made me conclude that it would be perfect for creating a Heavy Metal aesthetic for the brand.

    To pick up the colors, I tried to move away from the usual colors on websites of this genre, such as the combination of black and red. Therefore, I chose blue because it is a sober and cold color and contrasts nicely with the shade of black I chose.

    Usability Testing

    To validate the developed features and obtain user feedback, I carried out an unmoderated remote usability test using the Maze tool, which had 4 participants.

    To measure how easily users completed tasks, it was divided into the following criteria:

  • Success with difficulty
  • Success
  • Partial success
  • Failure
  • Time spent on task

    • findings and next steps

    In general, the features were well accepted by the users who could easily carry out the tasks, including those who do not usually access heavy metal blogs.

    Before starting the test, I asked two closed questions to better understand users' preferences. I believe I achieved this objective successfully, as it was clear that the features covered several points of interest for users.

    In the end, to improve the user experience and generate new ideas, I asked participants for feedback and received valuable suggestions from them.

    Therefore, following user feedback, I intend to create unique band profiles so that users can see album history, hit songs, curiosities, and concert dates. Furthermore, I will create a club of members, and they will be able to create posts and playlists to add to their respective pages.

    Conclusions

    As a huge rock and heavy metal enthusiast, this project was a thought-provoking and fun experience for me.

    Interviewing users and understanding their pain points made me strive to create the best possible results to benefit them and resolve their difficulties in navigating this type of blog.

    I realized that many users are not loyal to any heavy metal blog due to the lack of harmony in the design regarding visual identity and content.

    Therefore, I intended to create a platform that engages fans and makes them return to the site whenever they need to look for important information.

    Finally, if I could do something different, I would test it with more participants, especially those who actually access heavy metal blogs. However, during user testing, I also obtained valuable suggestions from people outside of this musical style.
